# Chapter 10: 思维的密码

## Core Idea
Human progress depends on symbolic systems: natural languages, writing, mathematics, programming, cryptography, artificial languages, and biological codes all extend what minds can discover and build.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Symbol system continuum**
  - When to use: Comparing natural language, math, programming, music, DNA, and AI.
  - How: Identify symbols, rules, compression, translation, and generativity.
- **Artificial-language experiment**
  - When to use: Researching language learning without uncontrolled natural-language variation.
  - How: Create controlled miniature languages and manipulate frequency, spelling, sound, or similarity.
- **Learning-begets-learning loop**
  - When to use: Explaining why multilinguals often learn later languages or symbolic systems faster.
  - How: Reuse overlap, strengthen control, and let gains in one system improve another.

## Key Concepts
- **Rosetta Stone**: Multilingual artifact that enabled decipherment.
- **Computer language**: Formal symbol system for instructing machines.
- **Mathematical language**: Symbol system for modeling, proof, and prediction.
- **Wug test**: Child language task showing rule generalization to novel words.

## Mental Models
- Treat every code as both a tool for thought and a constraint on thought.
- If a symbol system can compress complexity, it can accelerate discovery.

## Anti-patterns
- **Natural-language exceptionalism**: Ignoring parallels between language, math, code, and biology.
- **AI-language separation**: Treating artificial intelligence as independent from human symbol systems.

## Worked Example
Artificial languages such as Colbertian or Brocanto let researchers manipulate one property at a time. This is impossible in natural languages, where frequency, spelling, sound, meaning, and culture are entangled.

## Key Takeaways
1. Cryptography, writing, programming, and math are language-like systems that alter history.
2. Artificial languages help isolate mechanisms of language learning.
3. Mathematical notation accelerated science by making complex relations manipulable.
4. Multilingual and symbolic learning create compounding advantages.
