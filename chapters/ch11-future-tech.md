# Chapter 11: 科技的未来

## Core Idea
Future language technologies, brain interfaces, AI, and genetic knowledge will expand communication, but they require ethical wisdom and more inclusive science.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Thought-language boundary test**
  - When to use: Discussing brain-computer interfaces, animal cognition, infant cognition, or AI.
  - How: Ask what evidence is language-mediated and what remains when overt language is absent.
- **Dual-use technology audit**
  - When to use: Brain interfaces, AI translation, neural recording, genetic tools, or surveillance.
  - How: List benefits, abuses, consent risks, governance gaps, and who controls access.
- **Language diversity as discovery infrastructure**
  - When to use: Research design, funding, science policy, and knowledge access.
  - How: Include multilingual populations and non-English scholarship as sources of insight.

## Key Concepts
- **Brain-computer interface**: Technology that records, decodes, or stimulates neural activity for communication or control.
- **Neural privacy**: Protection against unauthorized capture or interpretation of neural activity.
- **WEIRD bias**: Overrepresentation of Western, educated, industrialized, rich, democratic samples.
- **Otoacoustic emissions**: Sounds generated by the ear; research suggests cognitive modulation.

## Mental Models
- Science gathers knowledge faster than society gathers wisdom; governance must catch up.
- Excluding multilingual people hides mechanisms that may be central to human cognition.

## Anti-patterns
- **Tech inevitability without ethics**: Building neural language tools without consent and social safeguards.
- **English-only science**: Treating untranslated knowledge as nonexistent.
- **Basic-science impatience**: Undervaluing research because its use is not yet obvious.

## Worked Example
Brain implants that translate neural activity into speech can restore communication, but the same direction of technology raises questions about thought privacy, consent, and misuse.

## Key Takeaways
1. Technology may blur language, thought, and machine communication.
2. The same tool can restore speech or invade privacy.
3. Multilingual inclusion can reveal hidden cognitive variables.
4. Language diversity has economic, scientific, and survival value.
