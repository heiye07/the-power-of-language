# Preface: 序言 欢迎来到语言世界

## Core Idea
Language is a force that organizes perception, memory, identity, culture, and social action. The book frames multilingualism as a way to see the mechanisms of language more clearly.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Language as access and barrier**
  - When to use: Analyzing communication breakdowns, social exclusion, translation risk, or persuasion.
  - How: Ask what language makes possible, what it hides, and who is included or excluded by it.
- **Label power**
  - When to use: Evaluating naming choices, political terms, identity labels, clinical categories, or product copy.
  - How: Compare how different labels change imagery, emotion, agency, and moral judgment.

## Key Concepts
- **Psycholinguistics**: The study of how language and mind interact.
- **Multilingual lens**: Using multilingual experience to expose language effects that are invisible in monolingual assumptions.
- **Language categories**: Mental labels that organize reality but may not match natural boundaries.

## Mental Models
- Think of language as a filter: it does not create all reality, but it changes what passes through quickly.
- Treat multilingual people as the norm for understanding human cognition, not as an exception.

## Anti-patterns
- **Monolingual default**: Assuming findings from monolingual speakers explain all minds.
- **Label neutrality**: Treating terms as simple containers rather than active frames.

## Worked Example
The preface uses cases such as grammatical gender, translation ambiguity, and bilingual benefits to show the same move: a small linguistic difference can shift perception, memory, emotion, or high-stakes action.

## Key Takeaways
1. Language is a cognitive tool, not only a communication channel.
2. Multilingualism reveals how flexible and context-sensitive the mind is.
3. Misreading or mistranslating language can have legal, political, clinical, and historical consequences.
4. Understanding language power makes people less vulnerable to manipulation.
