# Glossary

**Artificial language** - A deliberately constructed symbol system such as a programming language, Esperanto, Klingon, Brocanto, or a lab-made miniature language. (Ch 10)

**Bouba-kiki effect** - A sound-symbolism effect where people map rounded sounds to rounded shapes and sharp sounds to jagged shapes above chance. (Ch 9)

**Cognitive reserve** - Functional resilience that lets people maintain cognitive performance despite aging, disease, stress, or neural decline. (Ch 5)

**Cultural frame switching** - The shift in norms, self-construal, gestures, values, and expectations that can occur when a multilingual person switches language. (Ch 6)

**Executive function** - Cognitive control processes including attention, inhibition, switching, monitoring, and working memory. (Ch 5)

**Foreign language effect** - The tendency for non-native language contexts to reduce emotional immediacy and change moral, financial, medical, or risk decisions. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Grammatical gender** - A language system that classifies nouns into gender-like categories; it can influence memory, description, and mental representation. (Ch 8)

**Language attrition** - Partial loss or reduced accessibility of a previously known language, often after migration, adoption, or long disuse. (Epilogue)

**Language-dependent memory** - Improved memory retrieval when the language at recall matches the language active during encoding. (Ch 1, Ch 6)

**Linguistic relativity** - The idea that language influences, but does not fully determine, thought and perception. (Ch 3)

**McGurk effect** - A multisensory speech illusion in which mismatched auditory and visual speech cues produce a third perceived sound. (Ch 3)

**Metalinguistic awareness** - Awareness that words are symbols and that objects and names do not map one-to-one. (Ch 5)

**Neural reserve** - Structural and functional brain reinforcement, such as grey matter, white matter, and connectivity changes, that supports cognitive resilience. (Ch 5)

**Parallel activation** - Simultaneous activation of multiple languages, sounds, meanings, spellings, signs, or translations in a multilingual mind. (Ch 2)

**Sapir-Whorf hypothesis** - A family of claims about language shaping thought, ranging from strong determinism to weaker relativity. (Ch 3)

**Sound symbolism** - Non-arbitrary association between sound form and meaning or perception. (Ch 9)

**Speech register** - A language variety selected for context, audience, role, or social meaning. (Ch 7)

**Theory of mind** - The ability to attribute beliefs, intentions, and knowledge to oneself and others, including beliefs that differ from one's own. (Ch 5)

**Translation** - Re-creation of meaning across languages and cultures, not simple word replacement. (Ch 9)

**WEIRD sample** - Research populations that are Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and Democratic; overreliance on them limits generalization. (Ch 11)
